  - category: "General"
    icon: "fa-circle-info"
    items:
      - q: "What is RLadies+?"
        a: |
          RLadies+ is a worldwide organization whose mission is to promote gender diversity in the R community. We support minority genders — including cis/trans women, trans men, non-binary, genderqueer, and agender individuals — to reach their programming potential. With 200+ chapters across 60+ countries and over 100,000 members, we are one of the largest communities in the R ecosystem.
      - q: "Why the name change from R-Ladies to RLadies+?"
        a: |
          Our community told us that not everyone felt seen in the name "R-Ladies." After a [public discussion on GitHub](https://github.com/rladies/rladies.github.io/issues) running from April to November 2024, the Global Leadership Team decided on RLadies+. The `+` signals broader inclusivity while preserving our well-established identity. The new visual identity was created in collaboration with [Science Graphic Design](https://sciencegraphicdesign.com/).
      - q: "Who can participate in RLadies+?"
        a: |
          Anyone who identifies as a woman or gender minority and is interested in R is welcome. Our community includes newcomers learning their first lines of code, experienced package developers, conference speakers, educators, and industry professionals. Allies of all genders are welcome to attend events, though leadership roles and certain programs are reserved for minority genders.
      - q: "Is RLadies+ only for R users?"
        a: |
          While R is at our core, our community embraces the broader data science ecosystem. Many chapters host events covering Python, SQL, Git, statistics, and other tools alongside R. You do not need to be an experienced R user to join — beginners are very welcome.
      - q: "Is RLadies+ a registered organization?"
        a: |
          RLadies+ operates as a project under [The R Consortium](https://www.r-consortium.org/), a Linux Foundation project. Donations to RLadies+ are tax-deductible in the United States through the R Consortium's 501(c)(6) status.

  - category: "Chapters"
    icon: "fa-map-marker-alt"
    items:
      - q: "How do I find a local chapter?"
        a: |
          Visit our [Chapters page](/chapters/) to browse all active chapters on an interactive map and searchable table. You can filter by country or search by city. Most chapters also have a presence on [Meetup](https://www.meetup.com/pro/rladies/).
      - q: "How do I start a new chapter?"
        a: |
          We love seeing new chapters! Fill out the [chapter organiser form](https://airtable.com/appJMrFOBmOlnkUBo/shrN0hRVnFiMmcBiU) to get started. You'll be connected with the global team who will help you set up. Our [RLadies+ Guide](https://guide.rladies.org/) has detailed instructions on starting a chapter, running events, and finding speakers. You can also join our [mentoring program](/programs/mentoring/) to get paired with an experienced organiser.
      - q: "What happens at RLadies+ meetups?"
        a: |
          Formats vary by chapter but commonly include talks, workshops, tutorials, code-alongs, book clubs, and social networking events. Some chapters meet monthly, others less frequently. Events can be in-person, online, or hybrid. Check your local chapter's page for upcoming events.
      - q: "I'm the only organiser in my chapter. Can I get help?"
        a: |
          Absolutely. Our [mentoring program](/programs/mentoring/) pairs new or solo organisers with experienced ones who can help with finding speakers, choosing formats, boosting engagement, and more. You can also reach out on our Community Slack for support.

  - category: "Getting Involved"
    icon: "fa-hands-helping"
    items:
      - q: "How do I join the RLadies+ community?"
        a: |
          There are many ways to get involved:

          - **Join a local chapter** — find one on our [Chapters page](/chapters/)
          - **Join our Community Slack** — [sign up here](/form/community-slack/)
          - **Update your directory profile** — add yourself to the [Members directory](/directory/)
          - **Follow us** on [Mastodon](https://hachyderm.io/@RLadiesGlobal) or [Bluesky](https://bsky.app/profile/rladies.org)
          - **Attend events** — browse [upcoming events](/events/)
          - **Watch recordings** on our [YouTube channel](https://www.youtube.com/@RLadiesGlobal)
      - q: "Can I give a talk at an RLadies+ event?"
        a: |
          Yes! Chapters are always looking for speakers. Contact your local chapter directly or reach out on our Community Slack. Talks can be about anything related to R, data science, or your professional journey — all experience levels are welcome. If you'd like feedback on your abstract first, check out our [Abstract Review program](/form/abstract-request/).
      - q: "How can organizations support RLadies+?"
        a: |
          Organizations can support us in several ways:

          - **Sponsor a local chapter** — provide venue space, food, or beverages for events
          - **Host events** — offer your space for meetups or workshops
          - **Nominate speakers** — recommend RLadies+ members for conferences and talks
          - **Financial contributions** — [donate via PayPal](https://www.paypal.com/donate?hosted_button_id=8P5LKFNF6JRJ6)
          - **Promote internally** — encourage employees to attend and participate
      - q: "How can I contribute to the RLadies+ website or open source projects?"
        a: |
          Our website and many other projects are open source on [GitHub](https://github.com/rladies). Check out the issues labelled "help wanted" for good starting points. You can also write for our [blog](/form/blog-submission/) or contribute to the [RLadies+ Guide](https://guide.rladies.org/).

  - category: "Programs & Initiatives"
    icon: "fa-rocket"
    items:
      - q: "What is the mentoring program?"
        a: |
          The [mentoring program](/programs/mentoring/) connects new chapter organisers with experienced ones. Mentors help with common challenges like finding speakers, choosing event formats, and maintaining engagement. [Sign up here](/form/mentoring/) to be matched with a mentor.
      - q: "What is the Rotating Curator (RoCur)?"
        a: |
          The RoCur is our rotating Bluesky account ([@weare.rladies.org](https://bsky.app/profile/weare.rladies.org)) where a different community member takes over each week to share their work, interests, and experiences. It's a great way to spotlight minority gender R users and keep the community engaged. [Sign up to curate or nominate someone](/form/rocur/).
      - q: "What is the Abstract Review program?"
        a: |
          We have over 80 international reviewers who provide constructive feedback on conference abstracts and funding applications. Whether you're submitting to a major conference or applying for a grant, our reviewers can help strengthen your proposal. [Submit an abstract for review](/form/abstract-request/) or [become a reviewer](/form/abstract-reviewer/).
      - q: "How does the Community Slack work?"
        a: |
          Our Slack workspace is open to women and gender minorities interested in R. It's a space for asking questions, sharing resources, job postings, event announcements, and connecting with the global community. The [Code of Conduct](/coc/) applies to all Slack interactions. [Join here](/form/community-slack/).
      - q: "Can I write for the RLadies+ blog?"
        a: |
          Yes! We welcome posts about tutorials, community stories, event recaps, career journeys, and more. [Submit a blog proposal](/form/blog-submission/) and our editorial team will help you through the process.

  - category: "Code of Conduct"
    icon: "fa-shield-halved"
    items:
      - q: "What is the RLadies+ Code of Conduct?"
        a: |
          Our [Code of Conduct](/coc/) applies to all RLadies+ spaces — including meetups, social media, Slack, and online/offline events. We are dedicated to providing a harassment-free experience for everyone. The CoC covers offensive comments, unwelcome commentary, deliberate misgendering, unwelcome sexual attention, threats, stalking, and more.
      - q: "How do I report a Code of Conduct violation?"
        a: |
          If you experience or witness a violation, please email **reporting@rladies.org**. Reports are handled confidentially by the Global Leadership Team. We prioritise the safety of marginalised people in all decisions. You can also report directly to a local chapter organiser if you feel more comfortable doing so.
