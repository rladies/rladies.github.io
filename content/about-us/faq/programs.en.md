  - q: "What is the mentoring program?"
    a: |
      The [mentoring program](/programs/mentoring/) connects new chapter organisers with experienced ones. Mentors help with common challenges like finding speakers, choosing event formats, and maintaining engagement. [Sign up here](/form/mentoring-signup/) to be matched with a mentor.
  - q: "What is the Rotating Curator (RoCur)?"
    a: |
      The RoCur is our rotating Bluesky account ([@weare.rladies.org](https://bsky.app/profile/weare.rladies.org)) where a different community member takes over each week to share their work, interests, and experiences. It's a great way to spotlight minority gender R users and keep the community engaged. [Sign up to curate or nominate someone](/form/rocur-signup/).
  - q: "What is the Abstract Review program?"
    a: |
      We have over 80 international reviewers who provide constructive feedback on conference abstracts and funding applications. Whether you're submitting to a major conference or applying for a grant, our reviewers can help strengthen your proposal. [Submit an abstract for review](/form/abstract-request/) or [become a reviewer](/form/abstract-volunteer/).
  - q: "How does the Community Slack work?"
    a: |
      Our Slack workspace is open to women and gender minorities interested in R. It's a space for asking questions, sharing resources, job postings, event announcements, and connecting with the global community. The [Code of Conduct](/coc/) applies to all Slack interactions. [Join here](/form/community-slack/).
  - q: "Can I write for the RLadies+ blog?"
    a: |
      Yes! We welcome posts about tutorials, community stories, event recaps, career journeys, and more. [Submit a blog proposal](/form/blog-submission/) and our editorial team will help you through the process.
